What Your Customers Actually Experience

These are real exchanges from testing the live system — not mock-ups written for marketing. Two things are fixed and never change: the opening greeting, and the line acknowledging an emergency. Everything else adapts naturally to what the caller actually says, the same way a real person would.

Every conversation starts the same way, the instant a call is missed:
"Hi, this is the front desk for [Your Business Name]. I saw your call come in — how can we help?"

Example 1 — Catching an emergency from how someone actually talks

Emergency
Customer My AC is blowing hot air.
Frontdesk Thanks — I'm flagging this as urgent. I'm alerting the on-call tech now. Can I get the address where this is happening?
Customer 11815 Vance Jackson Rd., #804
Frontdesk Got it — that's logged, and the on-call tech already has your number and address. Someone will reach out shortly to get a tech out to you. Hang tight.

Notice the customer never said "no cooling" or any other obvious trigger word — they said their AC was doing the opposite of its job. Frontdesk caught the meaning, not just the keywords, and the owner's phone got an alert the moment it did.

Example 2 — A routine question that turns into a captured lead

Routine
Customer How often should I have my Heater/AC serviced?
Frontdesk Good question — most homeowners get the AC serviced before summer and the heater before winter, so twice a year keeps things running well and catches small problems early. Want me to get a tune-up scheduled for you?
Customer Do you handle heat pumps?
Frontdesk Yes, we work on heat pumps along with standard AC and furnace systems. Is that what you've got?

This customer never described a problem — just asked a general question. Frontdesk answered it briefly, then guided the conversation toward actually capturing the lead, without it feeling like a hard sales pivot.

Example 3 — Pricing questions never get a guessed number

Routine
Customer How much should I expect to pay for Heat/AC maintenance?
Frontdesk Good question — I don't have exact pricing in front of me right now, but I'll have someone follow up with the details. To get that started, is this for the AC, the heater, or both?

Frontdesk never invents a price. If you'd rather it quote your actual tune-up or diagnostic fees directly instead of deferring every pricing question, that's something we can configure for your account — just ask.
